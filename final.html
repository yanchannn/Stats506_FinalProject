Introduction

Energy conservation is a hot issue nowadays. Energy can be conserved by reducing wastage and losses, improving efficiency through technological upgrades and improved operation and maintenance. In this project, I am working on the problem:

Do buildings with electrical upgrades in the past year consume less electricity than those without recent upgrades?

Data / Methods

I use the data from the 2012 US Commercial Building Energy Consumption Survey (CBECS)

I will calculate the estimated mean building-level energy intensity of the electricity (bleu) which is defined as annual electricity consumption (ELCNS) / square foot (SQFT) for buildings with recent electrical upgrades (RENELC) and those without. The results are further stratified by the primary building activity (PBA) and geography (REGION).

I focus on five major building activity: office, warehouse, education, shopping, and service when doing the analysis since these five categories have more observations than others.

The first thing to do is to clean the data: select the observations in the five categories and remove rows with any missing value. Secondly, I use the statement PROC SURVEYMEANS to calculate the estimated mean energy intensity of electricity and calculate the 95% confidence intervals using the replicate weights (FINALWT1-197).

The source code can be accessed at my Github repo.
